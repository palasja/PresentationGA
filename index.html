Google Analytics hierarchy

Google Analytics is organized in a hierarchy which is made of

  • Organization (optional)
  • Analytics account
  • Analytics property
  • Data streams (Google Analytics 4 properties)
Organization (optional)

Products and users are collected together in organizations. An organization represents a company, and lets you access your company’s product accounts

Analytics account
The account is the gateway to Analytics, and provides the container for your Analytics properties. You can have one or more Analytics accounts (up to a maximum of 100). Each account can contain up to 100 properties.
Analytics property
That Properties are the containers for your reports based on the data you collect from your apps and sites. You can create up to 100 properties in an account.
Data streams
Each Google Analytics 4 property can have up to 50 data streams. A data stream is a flow of data from a customer touchpoint (e.g., app, website) to Analytics. When you create a data stream, Analytics generates a snippet of code that you add to your app or site to collect that data. Data is collected from the time you add the code, and that data forms the basis of your reports.
All this things created and configured in you google analytics account.

Evetnts

Evetnts - that what measure user interactions on a website or app. Most of the events that users trigger on your website or app are not sent one at a time. Instead, most events are grouped together (or batched).

Types of events

  • Automatically collected events
  • Enhanced measurement events
  • Recommended events
  • Custom events

Reason for use

  • Free
  • Automatic collection of data
  • You can create customization reports
  • Easy integration with other tools and platforms
